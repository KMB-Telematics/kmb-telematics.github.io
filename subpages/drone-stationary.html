Stationary Drone Detection

Stationary or orbiting drones flying at a constant range cannot be seen with legacy radars currently used for drone detection. KMB's unique imaging radar approach can detect these targets for the first time. This closes a detection gap in counter-drone / counter-UAS systems that threats are quite literally able to fly through.

KMB's unique imaging radar approach can see autonomous GPS-guided / non-RF emitting drones in all weather, day or night. The sensor provides rich radar imagery that can be processed with machine learning (ML) / AI to classify targets, reject false positives, or to fuse with other sensors such as cameras / EO/IR.

Current-generation radars filter out slow-moving objects to reduce clutter and compensate for the radar's low resolution. KMB radar has high resolution and performs full-scene imaging. This allows KMB radar to see an unlimited number of targets, whether they are moving or stationary. This makes KMB radar perfect for detecting swarms of even stationary drones.
